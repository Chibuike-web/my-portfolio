## Style Prompt

MediBridge should feel like clinical infrastructure with a human pulse: precise, calm, trustworthy, and built for urgent hospital workflows. The promo uses product UI as the hero, with large editorial typography and restrained medical-interface motion. The visual language should suggest paper records becoming searchable patient data, not generic blue healthcare software.

## Colors

- `#f4fbf8` Canvas: clean hospital daylight
- `#10231f` Ink: primary text and dark panels
- `#1d7f68` Clinical green: primary accent
- `#d7efe6` Soft mint: interface fields and highlights
- `#f06449` Alert coral: urgency and transfer emphasis

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, heavy weights for high-trust editorial claims
- System/body: `Arial`, light and medium weights for product labels

## Motion

- Primary transition: blur crossfade with a slight focus pull
- Accent transition: directional push for the transfer scene
- Entrances: staggered text, product frames lifting into place, scanning lines and status chips entering after the core claim

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use neon blue, purple tech gradients, or abstract hospital stock imagery.
- Do not hide the product behind decorative shapes.
- Do not over-animate clinical UI; it should feel controlled and legible.
- Do not use tiny web text sizes that fail in video.
